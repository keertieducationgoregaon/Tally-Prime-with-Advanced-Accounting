1. Introduction

Bill-wise Details & Credit Limits give you complete control over customer and supplier accounts:

  • Track every invoice separately
  • Know exactly which bills are pending
  • Adjust advances automatically
  • Prevent over-credit sales
  • Generate professional statements

2. Theoretical Concepts

2.1 Bill-wise Details – 4 Reference Types

TypeWhen to UseExample
New RefCreating fresh invoiceSales Invoice SI-001
Against RefPayment against old billAdjusting receipt to SI-001
AdvanceMoney received before invoiceCustomer pays ₹1L in advance
On AccountPayment without bill referenceCustomer pays ₹50K (not specified)

2.3 Credit Limits

Maximum credit allowed to a customer.
Tally warns when limit is crossed (does not stop voucher).

3. Tally Prime Configuration

Enable Bill-wise Details:
Gateway → F11 → Accounting Features → Maintain Bill-wise Details: Yes

Credit Limits → Set directly in Customer Ledger (No F11 required)

4–5. Practical Tutorial – Bill-wise Details (Using ABC TRADERS)

5.1 Sales Invoice – New Reference
10 Laptops @ ₹50,000 → ₹5,00,000
Bill Ref: SI001 (New Ref)
5.2 Receipt – Against Reference
₹2,00,000 received → Adjust against SI001
Outstanding → ₹3,00,000
5.3 Advance Receipt
₹1,00,000 advance → Ref: ADV001
5.4 Sales Invoice with Advance Adjustment
8 Laptops → ₹4,00,000 (New Ref: SI002)
Adjust ₹1,00,000 from ADV001
Net Receivable → ₹3,00,000
5.5 On Account Receipt
₹50,000 received → No specific bill

6. Credit Limits – Practical

Set Credit Limit:
Alter → Customer1 → Credit Limit: ₹6,00,000

Test Exceeding Limit:
Current outstanding ₹5L + New invoice ₹2L = ₹7L → Warning Appears

7. Professional Business Examples

Trading → Monthly credit sales + partial payments
Manufacturing → Supplier credit + debit notes
Services → Project advances + milestone billing

8. Reports & Verification

  • Receivables / Payables → Bill-wise, Ageing, Overdue
  • Ledger Outstandings → Shows advances & on-account
  • Customers exceeding credit limit → Highlighted
